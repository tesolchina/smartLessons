A Policy That Changed Everything

In September 2017, Hong Kong Baptist University made a bold decision that would transform teaching and learning. What seemed like a simple attendance policy change became the catalyst for educational innovation.

South China Morning Post Letter

Published September 1, 2017 ‚Ä¢ Reading Time: 2 minutes

"Relaxed attendance rules at Hong Kong's Baptist University will improve teaching quality"

As a lecturer at Hong Kong Baptist University's Language Centre, I support the university's recent decision to relax attendance requirements for all its students.

Despite its potential impact on students' class participation, this new policy may improve the overall quality of teaching and stimulate innovation among teaching staff at the university.

The Policy Transformation

Old Policy (Pre-2017)

Strict 30% Rule

Students missing more than 30% of classes would automatically fail the course

Consequences:

  • Guaranteed attendance, but not engagement
  • Teachers relied on mandatory presence
  • Limited innovation incentive
‚úì

New Policy (2017)

Flexible Attendance

Students can pass with less-than-perfect attendance records

Opportunities:

  • Quality over quantity approach
  • Teachers must innovate to attract students
  • Student autonomy and responsibility

The Philosophy Behind the Change

Student Empowerment

"University students are young adults who should learn to make responsible decisions on how to spend their time. If they find a better way to study course materials than attending lectures, the university should respect their choices."

Teaching Innovation

"Under the new policy, all university faculty members must rethink how to attract students with more effective teaching, as their attendance is no longer guaranteed."

Historical Context

The Harvard Origin

Professor Eric Mazur first adopted the flipped classroom approach at Harvard University in the 1990s. This innovative method has recently gained much currency in higher education worldwide.

Through video lectures that cover basic course material, classroom time with instructors can be saved for more demanding tasks that involve conceptual understanding and problem solving.

Perfect for Language Teaching

  • Students can practice language skills more autonomously
  • Receive guidance from tutors to improve independent learning
  • Focus on communication and interaction during class time
