🔍 Scanning Skills Challenge - Information Hunt!

Time to practice scanning - the superpower of finding specific information quickly! �

📚 Remember: Scanning means moving your eyes quickly to find specific information, NOT reading for understanding. Think of it like using a metal detector - you're looking for treasure!

🎯 Information Hunt Challenges

🔢 Challenge 1: Sample Size Detective

What is the sample size of this study?

  1. 245 participants
  2. 356 participants
  3. 423 participants
  4. 512 participants

Your mission: Find the answer AND tell us:

  • Which section you found it in
  • What keywords helped you locate it quickly
  • How long it took you to find (be honest!)

🔬 Challenge 2: Research Method Hunter

What was the primary data collection method used in this study?

  1. Face-to-face interviews
  2. Direct observations
  3. Online questionnaire/survey
  4. Laboratory experiments

Your mission: Explain your scanning strategy:

  • What search terms did you use?
  • Did you use Ctrl+F (Cmd+F)? What words?
  • Which section was most helpful?

📊 Challenge 3: Statistical Significance Sleuth

The article reports statistical significance. Which significance level appears most frequently?

  1. p < 0.05
  2. p < 0.01
  3. p < 0.001
  4. All of the above appear

Your mission: Share your evidence:

  • Copy and paste ONE example sentence showing significant results
  • Explain where you found the most statistical information
  • What made these results easy/hard to spot?

💡 Scanning Power Tips:

  • 🔍 Use search functions: Ctrl+F (or Cmd+F) is your best friend!
  • 📍 Look for numbers: Statistics often stand out visually
  • 🎯 Target key sections: Methods for procedures, Results for data
  • 👀 Scan for keywords: "participants," "survey," "significant," etc.
