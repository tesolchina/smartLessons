💉 Flu Shot Participation Analysis

GCAP 3226: Empowering Citizens through Data

Topic 6 • Group Work (4-6 students) • Public Health Data Governance & Policy Analysis

🎯 Project Overview

Analyze flu vaccination participation patterns in Hong Kong, building upon historical research to provide updated analysis of vaccination barriers, demographic trends, and policy effectiveness in the post-COVID era.

This project offers an exciting opportunity to evaluate data governance practices in public health policy. You'll examine how vaccination data is collected, reported, and used for policy decisions, while exploring innovative approaches like nasal spray vaccines and their potential impact on participation rates.

🚀 Substantial Research Foundation Available

📝 Three Published Research Letters

We have extensive published research foundation with three comprehensive letters in the South China Morning Post:

Letter 1: "Hong Kong schools could do more to promote flu shots" (Jan 2018)
"According to Department of Health data, about 65,000 primary schoolchildren received flu shots in the 2016-17 flu season, so only 18.7 per cent of the 349,008 primary schoolchildren were protected through the subsidy vaccination programmes... To hold school principals accountable for promoting flu vaccinations, the bureau should collect data about vaccination administration."
📖 Read Full Letter
Letter 2: "Hong Kong's seasonal flu vaccine programmes need better marketing" (Nov 2017)
"During the 2016/17 flu season, more than 700,000 individuals in Hong Kong have received free or subsidised vaccinations... Considering the low participation rate of the GVP and VSS combined (about 29 per cent), the department should devote more resources to marketing its vaccination programmes."
📖 Read Full Letter
Letter 3: "How Hong Kong can better protect children from the flu" (Jun 2018)
"According to a recent study led by the University of Hong Kong, influenza vaccination could reduce infection rates by 65 per cent for children aged six months to 17 years... To more accurately assess vaccination effectiveness, the Department of Health should create an influenza record file for every child in the city."
