🗂️ Municipal Solid Waste Charging Scheme

GCAP 3226: Empowering Citizens through Data

Topic 4 • Group Work (4-6 students) • Data Governance & Public Policy Analysis

🎯 Project Overview

Examine Hong Kong's Municipal Solid Waste Charging Scheme through a comprehensive data governance lens, analyzing both policy implementation and public response.

Teams will evaluate the transparency, accessibility, and effectiveness of waste management data collection practices across different stakeholders including government agencies, universities, and residential communities.

We have already published two comprehensive letters in the South China Morning Post examining waste management data governance:

Letter 1 - Universities should disclose data on waste recycling
"We urge the government to require all publicly funded tertiary institutions to disclose data about waste recycling... As university dorms will be included in the upcoming waste charging scheme, the Environmental Protection Department should require all publicly funded universities to report the waste recycling performance of dorm residents."
📖 Read Full Letter
Letter 2 - Collect deeper waste data to promote recycling
"Our case study of a private residential community in Tai Po reveals recycling rates of 0.29% for plastics, 0.9% for paper, 4.55% for glass, and 0.99% for metal... The Environmental Protection Department should make it compulsory for all residential communities to collect and publicise solid waste data."
