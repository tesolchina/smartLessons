1 Stage 1: Topic Exploration

🔍 Identify Your Policy Focus

Week 3-4 | Choose a specific public affairs issue

✓ Select a Specific Decision: Choose a concrete policy decision (e.g., "Bus frequency adjustment for Route 272A" NOT "transport policy in general")
✓ Gather Background Information: Collect reports from Legislative Council, government websites, media coverage
✓ Assess Current Data Use: Look for evidence of how data influences the policy decision

📋 Week 5 Milestone - Topic Finalization:

  • 📋 Clear research question (1-2 sentences) - Group consultation format
  • 📋 Background summary (1-2 pages)
  • 📋 Initial data assessment

2 Stage 2: Data Preparation & Collection

📊 Gather and Evaluate Data

Week 6-7 | Find, request, and collect relevant data

✓ Explore Open Data: Search data.gov.hk for relevant datasets and evaluate their usefulness
✓ Request Government Data: Email relevant departments with specific, professional requests (≤2 questions per group) - Must send by Week 6!
✓ Collect Field Data: Conduct surveys, observations, or stakeholder interviews to supplement official data
⚠️ Important: Inform teachers of your fieldwork plans - they may join if time permits!

📋 Week 7 Milestone - Data Collection:

  • 📋 Data inventory table
  • 📋 Government request emails (sent by Week 6)
  • 📋 Field data collection results

3 Stage 3: Data Analysis & Recommendations

📈 Analyze and Recommend

Week 8-10 | Turn data into insights and policy recommendations

✓ Exploratory Data Analysis (Week 8): Create visualizations, check for patterns, outliers, and data quality issues
✓ Statistical Modeling (Week 8-9): Apply regression analysis or simulation to understand relationships and test hypotheses
✓ Policy Evaluation (Week 9): Assess current data governance practices and identify gaps in decision-making support
✓ Develop Recommendations (Week 9-10): Propose concrete improvements for data collection, analysis, and policy implementation

📋 Week 10 Milestone - Draft Report:

  • 📋 Complete draft report outline
  • 📋 Data analysis results and visualizations
  • 📋 Policy recommendations framework

4 Stage 4: Presentation & Final Submission

🎤 Present and Finalize

Week 11 | Presentation rehearsal and final report completion

✓ Presentation Preparation: Create slides, practice delivery, prepare for Q&A session
✓ Peer Review & Feedback: Rehearsal sessions with feedback from instructors and classmates
✓ Final Report Polish: Incorporate feedback, finalize formatting, and complete all sections
✓ Individual Reflections: Complete individual reflection essays on the project experience

📋 Final Deliverables:

  • 📋 Complete final report (group submission)
  • 📋 Group presentation (Week 11)
  • 📋 Individual reflection essays

⚠️ Important Reminders

Timeline is Tentative: Specific dates and deadlines will be confirmed during group consultations. Use this as a planning guide.

Government Data Requests: Must be sent by Week 6 at the latest! Government responses can take 3+ weeks, so early submission is critical.

Be Specific: Focus on concrete policy decisions rather than broad topics. "Bus stop spacing analysis" is better than "transportation policy."

Milestone Assessment: Expected outputs will be assessed during group consultations. These serve as progress checkpoints, not formal submissions.

Document Everything: Keep track of all data sources, requests sent, and methods used. This documentation is crucial for your final report.
