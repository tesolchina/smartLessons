📰 The Journey Since 2017

From SCMP Letter to AI Revolution

Let's revisit the SCMP letter from 2017 - 8 years ago:

Foundation: "I will experiment with the flipped classroom approach... I have created a series of video lectures students will watch in advance."

The vision was clear, but the tools weren't ready yet.

💡 Three Critical Developments

From 2017 Vision to 2025 Reality

🎥 From Video Creation Barriers to AI-Assisted Production

In 2017: Creating "a series of video lectures" took hours of manual work

Today: AI tools like HeyGen transform 4-6 hour processes into 5-10 minute workflows

🤖 From WhatsApp Groups to 24/7 AI Teaching Assistants

In 2017: Used WhatsApp to "stay connected beyond classrooms"

Today: AI streaming avatars provide constant, intelligent student support

🚀 From Encouraging Innovation to Democratizing Technology

In 2017: Called for "more resources to encourage faculty members"

Today: AI tools make flipped classroom accessible to every educator

🏛️ Perfect Timing: GE Office Support

New Policies Align with AI Solutions

🎯 The GE Office has recently introduced new policies to actively promote flipped classroom adoption:

Current GE Office Initiatives:

  • 💰 Funding Support: Financial resources now available for flipped classroom implementation
  • ⚖️ 50/50 Teaching Model: Year 1 GE teachers encouraged to allocate 50% classroom time to flipped materials
  • 🏢 Institutional Backing: Official recognition and encouragement of innovative practices

Integration Opportunities with AI Tools:

  • AI-Generated Content (Pre-Class): Personalized videos and materials
  • Small Group Facilitation (In-Class 50%): Focus on discussion and problem-solving
  • AI Avatar Support (Ongoing): 24/7 bridge between study and class activities

⚡ The Traditional Challenge

Why Flipped Classroom Adoption Has Been Limited

💰 Cost of Shooting and Editing Videos

  • Professional equipment and software costs
  • 4-6 hours per video production time
  • Technical skill requirements
  • Expensive maintenance and updates

📺 Making Videos Interactive and Engaging

  • Static content lacks engagement mechanisms
  • No real-time feedback or personalization
  • Difficulty tracking student progress
  • One-size-fits-all limitations

🔄 Scalability and Sustainability

  • High production costs limit content creation
  • Full re-production needed for updates
  • Language barriers for international students
  • Lack of 24/7 support
