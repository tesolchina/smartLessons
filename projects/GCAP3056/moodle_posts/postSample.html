⚠️ Reality Check: Anticipating Disappointing Answers

🎯 It's Not Free Lunch - Think Strategically!

Important: Only request information when it can genuinely help develop your arguments and benefit society. Government resources are limited and should be used wisely.

💭 Before Sending: Ask Yourself

  • 🔍 Purpose: How will the answers help develop your arguments?
  • 🏛️ Societal Benefit: Will this research contribute to public good?
  • 📊 Alternative Sources: Is this information available elsewhere?
  • 🎯 Specificity: Are you asking for actionable, usable data?

😞 Common Disappointing Outcomes

📭 Scenario 1: "No Information Available"

Response: "The requested data has not been collected or does not exist in our records."

Research Opportunity: This becomes a data governance argument - why isn't this crucial information being collected? This absence of data can be part of your findings!

🚫 Scenario 2: "Cannot Provide Information"

Response: "Disclosing this information may harm or prejudice [law enforcement/public interest/etc.]"

Research Opportunity: This becomes a transparency argument - examine the balance between secrecy and public accountability!

📰 Real Case Study: Facial Recognition Technology Request

🔍 Learning from a Rejected Request

Source: South China Morning Post, October 2019

📋 The Request:

The author submitted a Code on Access to Information application to Hong Kong Police Force regarding their use of automated facial recognition technology.

❌ The Response:

"The Hong Kong Police Force rejected the application on the grounds that disclosing the information may harm or prejudice law enforcement."

🎯 The Research Value:

Even though the request was rejected, this outcome became valuable research data about:

  • Transparency Issues: Government reluctance to disclose surveillance practices
  • Accountability Gaps: Public's right to know vs. law enforcement secrecy
  • Policy Analysis: Need for clearer guidelines on surveillance technology use
💡 Key Lesson: Rejection ≠ Failure

The researcher turned this rejection into a powerful argument about the need for greater transparency in police use of surveillance technology. The absence of information became the presence of a critical finding!

📊 How to Use "Disappointing" Responses

  1. 🔍 Data Governance Angle: Analyze why certain data isn't collected
  2. 🏛️ Transparency Analysis: Examine what information is considered "sensitive"
  3. 📝 Policy Recommendations: Suggest improvements to information access
  4. 🌍 Comparative Study: How do other jurisdictions handle similar requests?
  5. ⚖️ Legal Framework: Are the rejection grounds justified and consistent?

⏰ Critical Timing Information (Missing from Video!)

📅 Official Response Timeframes

Response Type Timeframe What to Expect
📨 Interim Reply 10 calendar days Acknowledgment + estimated completion time
📄 Substantive Reply 21 calendar days Full response with requested information
⚠️ Exceptional Cases Up to 7 weeks Complex requests requiring extensive search/consultation
⚡ Pro Tip: Avoid the 7-Week Delay!

Break down complex requests into smaller, focused enquiries with maximum 3 questions each to stay within the 21-day timeframe.

🎯 Strategic Approach: The "3-Question Rule"

📝 Smart Request Structure

❌ Avoid This: Complex Request (Likely 7-week delay)

"Please provide all data on emergency alert system effectiveness, implementation costs, public feedback, technical specifications, staff training requirements, maintenance schedules, budget allocations, and future expansion plans for the past 5 years."

✅ Do This: Focused Request (21-day response)

Request 1: "Please provide: 1) Emergency alert system effectiveness data for 2023-2024, 2) Public response rates to alerts, 3) Technical performance metrics."

Request 2: "Please provide: 1) Implementation costs for emergency alert system, 2) Annual maintenance budget, 3) Staff training expenditure."

Request 3: "Please provide: 1) Future expansion plans, 2) Upcoming system upgrades, 3) Budget projections for next 2 years."

🚀 Benefits of the Strategic Approach:

  • ⚡ Faster Response: 21 days instead of 7 weeks
  • 📈 Higher Success Rate: Easier for departments to process
  • 🔄 Continuous Flow: Get information while waiting for next response
  • 🎯 Better Quality: More focused, detailed answers
  • 📊 Project Management: Align with your project timeline

📧 Professional Email Template

✉️ Use This Template Structure

Subject: Request for Information under Code on Access to Information - [Topic] - [University] Student Research


Dear Access to Information Officer,


I am writing to request information under the Code on Access to Information for academic research purposes.


Student Details:
Name: [Your Name]
University: [University Name]
Course: [Course Title]
Project Title: [Project Title]


Information Requested:
1. [Specific question 1]
2. [Specific question 2]
3. [Specific question 3]


Purpose: This information will be used solely for academic research and will contribute to [brief project description].


Timeframe: If possible, we would appreciate receiving this information within the standard 21-day period to align with our project timeline.


Thank you for your assistance with our research.


Yours sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Email Address]
[Student ID]

🎯 Key Success Factors:

  • 🔍 Specificity: Clearly define what information you need
  • 📅 Time Boundaries: Specify relevant time periods
  • 🎓 Academic Context: Emphasize educational purpose
  • 💼 Professional Tone: Use formal, respectful language
  • 📊 Clear Numbering: Number your questions for easy reference

👥 Mandatory Review Process

🚫 NEVER Send Requests Without Review!

Poorly crafted requests can delay your entire project and waste valuable government resources.

✅ Required Steps Before Sending:

  1. 🤝 Team Discussion:
    • Review questions with all team members
    • Ensure questions align with project objectives
    • Check for overlaps or gaps in information needs
    • Prioritize questions by importance
  2. 👨‍🏫 Teacher Consultation:
    • Submit draft requests via Moodle reply
    • Get feedback on question clarity and scope
    • Ensure compliance with academic standards
    • Verify alignment with course objectives
  3. 🔧 Final Refinement:
    • Incorporate feedback from team and teachers
    • Double-check the 3-question limit
    • Ensure professional language and tone
    • Include proper identification and project context
