BIO

Dr. Emma H. Zhang joined Hong Kong Baptist University in 2014, after obtaining her PhD in Literary Studies in the Chinese University of Hong Kong. Before joining the Language Centre, she worked at the College of International Education for eight years. She taught a range of courses including Academic Writing, Creative Expressions Across Cultures, Literary Appreciation, Reading Contemporary Worlds, and English Enhancement. Her research interests include comparative literature, comparative mythology, virtual exchange and e-learning. She has written on the subjects of Contemporary Asian American Literature, Chinese Mythology, as well as Life Writing. Her articles can be seen on Life Writing; Ex-position; Cha, An Asian Literary Journal; Hong Kong Review of Books; and MercatorNet. She has also conducted projects in E-learning and Virtual Exchange and serves as a Global Connect programme facilitator with Soliya, a leading organization in the field of Virtual Exchange.
