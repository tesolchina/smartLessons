Week 1 - Module 1: Data Governance in Everyday Life

Icebreaker: True/False Lead-In (5-10 minutes)

Story/Case: Imagine you're waiting for a bus in your neighborhood. You check a bus app, but it shows two stops with the same name on opposite sides of the street. You pick the wrong one and miss your bus! This happened in a real community due to poor data labeling.

True/False Questions: Consider these statements and decide if they are true or false.

  • T/F: Small data issues, like unclear bus stop names in an app, can cause real inconvenience to people. (Answer: True)
  • T/F: Data problems like this are only the responsibility of app developers and not the government or bus companies. (Answer: False)

Quick Chat: Think about this: "Has something like this ever happened to you with an app or service? How did it feel?" Share your thoughts with the class or a small group.

Presentation: Key Points on Data Governance (10-15 minutes)

  • What is Data Governance? It’s about ensuring data is clean, accurate, and transparent so it’s useful for everyone.
  • Why It Matters: Poor data (e.g., duplicate bus stop names) leads to confusion and inefficiency in daily life.
  • Real Case: In one community, residents wrote to the transport department and escalated the issue to senior officials until the bus company added unique codes to distinguish stops in the app.
  • Big Picture: Clean data isn’t just for convenience—it’s a foundation for better public services and trust in systems.

Delivery Tip: This section is for instructors to discuss or for students to read. Relate back to the icebreaker story for context.
